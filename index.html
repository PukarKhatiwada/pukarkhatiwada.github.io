Who I am

A developer rooted
in craft & precision.

Hey, I'm Pukar — a frontend developer based in Ilam, a small hill town in eastern Nepal that's mostly known for its tea gardens and foggy mornings. I've spent enough time here that patience just sort of rubbed off on me, and it shows in how I build things.

My foundation is HTML & CSS — I like knowing the raw materials well before reaching for a framework to speed things up. Good structure and styling first, shortcuts second.

Mostly, I just want the sites I build to feel right: fast, accessible, and comfortable to use whether you're on a laptop or a five-year-old phone. Pixel-perfect landing page or a messy layout problem, I'll dig in until it's solid.

✦   How I Work   ✦

Process

From brief
to launch.

Understand

A short call or message thread to get clear on what the site needs to do, who it's for, and what "done" looks like.

Structure

Content and layout first — sitemap, sections, hierarchy — before a single line of CSS gets written.

Build

Clean, semantic HTML and CSS, tested across real breakpoints and devices as it comes together, not just at the end.

Launch & support

Deployed, checked, and handed over — with room to come back for tweaks or updates after launch.

✦   Questions   ✦

Good to know

Frequently asked.

What do you actually build with?+

Hand-written HTML, CSS and JavaScript as the foundation — no page builders. Frameworks come in only when a project genuinely needs one.

Do you take on small projects?+

Yes — landing pages, small business sites, and single-page builds are all welcome, not just large ones.

How long does a typical build take?+

Depends on scope, but most small-to-medium sites move from brief to launch within a couple of weeks once content is ready.

What happens after launch?+

I stay reachable for fixes, small edits, or content updates — a site shouldn't go stale the day it ships.
